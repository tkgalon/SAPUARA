# About The Project

SAPUARA (Sapu-sapu Urban Alert & Response AI) is a project developed for the Microsoft Elevate x Dicoding Datathon under the theme:

> Urban Resilience & Smart City

This project focuses on the issue of invasive sapu-sapu fish (*Pterygoplichthys sp.*) in Indonesian urban rivers and their potential impact on river ecosystems and infrastructure.

Currently, the repository mainly contains:
- dataset collection and preparation,
- initial research exploration,
- and early-stage AI development planning.

The long-term goal of SAPUARA is to build an AI system capable of:
- detecting invasive sapu-sapu fish from river imagery,
- identifying burrow areas near river embankments,
- and supporting risk-based urban river monitoring.

---

# Background

In recent years, the population of invasive sapu-sapu fish in urban rivers has increased significantly. Besides disrupting ecosystems, these fish are known for creating burrows along riverbanks that may contribute to infrastructure degradation and erosion.

Several reports and environmental observations also indicate:
- worsening river pollution conditions,
- increasing invasive species dominance,
- and rising concerns regarding urban flood resilience.

SAPUARA was initiated as an exploratory AI project to study how computer vision and data-driven approaches could support smarter environmental monitoring systems in the future.

# Planned Features

The project roadmap currently includes:

## Fish Detection System
Object detection model for identifying:
- sapu-sapu fish,
- burrow/hole areas,
- and river environment conditions.

Planned technologies:
- YOLO
- PyTorch
- OpenCV

---

## Risk Scoring System
Tabular risk modeling for estimating riverbank vulnerability using:
- burrow density,
- rainfall data,
- and river segment information.

Planned technologies:
- CatBoost
- LightGBM
- XGBoost

---

## Inference API (Planned)
A future FastAPI-based inference service for image prediction and risk analysis.
